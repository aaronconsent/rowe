Timber prices per ton Texas

Updated June 2026 · By Bob Rowe, Rowe Land, Timber & Dozer Services

Current per-ton stumpage prices in Texas: pine sawtimber $22–$32/ton, pine chip-n-saw $14–$22/ton, pine pulpwood $8–$14/ton, hardwood sawtimber $22–$45/ton, hardwood pulpwood $5–$10/ton. These are at-the-stump prices (what landowners receive), not delivered-to-mill prices.

Per-ton prices are what most Texas timber sales settle on today. Older “lump-sum” sales (one negotiated total for the whole stand) still happen but are less common because per-ton pays the landowner for actual volume sold.

East Texas-specific factors

East Texas — the “Piney Woods” — sits in a strong pine market with multiple mills. Per-ton stumpage is usually at or above statewide averages. See East Texas pine prices →

What moves the number

  • Tract size — bigger tracts get more bidders
  • Road access — paved vs. dirt, condition
  • Distance to mill — direct cost on every truck
  • Tree quality — straight, large, defect-free pays more
  • Mill demand at sale time — housing starts, paper demand

Updated quarterly based on Texas A&M Forest Service stumpage reports, prevailing mill prices, and recent local sales.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert tons to dollars for my stand?
Multiply tons per acre × per-ton price × acres. A 30-year-old pine plantation often holds 100–150 tons per acre.
Why is stumpage less than delivered price?
Delivered price (what the mill pays the buyer at the mill gate) includes cutting, hauling, and the logger/buyer's margin. Stumpage is what's left for the landowner.
How current are these ranges?
Updated quarterly. Markets move; check back or call for the current quote for your area.
Are these prices the same across Texas?
Roughly, with East Texas typically at or above the statewide range because of mill density and the Piney Woods pine market.
